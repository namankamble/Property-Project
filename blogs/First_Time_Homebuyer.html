The First-Time Homebuyer’s Checklist: 7 Steps to a Smooth Purchase

DG

By Dhiraj Gajbhije

October 2, 2025 · 7 min read

Buying your first home is one of the most **exciting milestones** of your life—but it can also feel overwhelming without proper guidance. From saving for a down payment to closing the deal, each step plays a key role in making your home-buying journey smooth and stress-free. Here’s a checklist to help you navigate the process with confidence.

1. Define Your Budget Early

Before browsing listings, determine how much home you can afford. Use the **28/36 rule**—your mortgage payment should not exceed 28% of your monthly income, and total debt should stay under 36%. Factor in taxes, insurance, and maintenance costs to get a realistic picture.

2. Check Your Credit Score

Your credit score directly impacts your mortgage interest rate. Review your credit report and clear outstanding debts if possible. A score above 750 can significantly improve your chances of securing favorable loan terms.

3. Save for a Down Payment and Closing Costs

Most first-time buyers aim for a **20% down payment**, but many loan programs now allow as little as 5–10%. Don’t forget to set aside funds for **closing costs**, which typically range from 2–5% of the purchase price.

4. Get Pre-Approved for a Mortgage

A mortgage pre-approval gives you a clear understanding of your buying power and shows sellers you’re serious. It also helps you act quickly when you find your dream home, avoiding delays in competitive markets.

5. Work with a Trusted Real Estate Agent

Partnering with an experienced agent—like one from **Dhiraj Properties**—can make a huge difference. A good agent understands the local market, negotiates effectively, and guides you through each step of the transaction.

6. Schedule Inspections & Appraisals

Always schedule a professional **home inspection** before finalizing your purchase. This step ensures there are no hidden issues that could lead to expensive repairs later. Your lender will also require an **appraisal** to confirm the property’s market value.

7. Review Documents Carefully Before Closing

The final stage involves signing multiple legal documents. Review all terms—especially your interest rate, loan conditions, and payment schedules—before signing. Once everything is clear, congratulations—you’re a homeowner!

Final Thoughts

The journey to owning your first home can be smoother than you think with the right plan. Stay focused, be patient, and don’t rush decisions. Your dream home is out there—it just needs the right approach to find it.
